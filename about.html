Seattle Vehicle Collisions

Map About

Overview

The purpose of this smart dashboard is to visualize vehicle collisions across Seattle from 2004-2026. The map includes three visualizations: a cluster map, a heat map, and a point map. Using the timescale and severity level dropdown, users can filter and explore patterns across the data. An analytics tab provides additional visualizations that summarize the severity and distribuation of the collision data. Ultimately, the goal of this project is not only to provide an interactive tool for understanding road safety and management in Seattle, but to also help guide policy decisions and resource allocation in hopes of preventing future collisions.

The Data

The data used for this project was sourced from Seattle Department of Transportation's open GeoData portal. It includes information on all reported collisions in Seattle with locations, dates, times, severity levels, people involved, accident types, and more. The data was cleaned and cut down to only include collisions from 2025, and then processed into GeoJSON format for use in our smart dashboard project. The basemap is provided by Mapbox.

Team

The smart dashboard was developed through the collaborative efforts of Group 16 in the course GEOG 458: Advanced Digital Geographies at the University of Washington. Project requirements and guidelines were provided by the course instructor Bo Zhao. No AI was used in the making of this project.
